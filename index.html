About Richard

Richard Matthew Stallman, often known by his initials RMS, is a pioneering figure in the free software movement. His work has fundamentally shaped how we think about software freedom, user rights, and collaborative development in the digital age.

As the founder of the GNU Project in 1983, Stallman initiated the creation of a completely free operating system. His vision was revolutionary: software should respect users' freedom to run, study, modify, and share it. This philosophy became the foundation of the modern open source movement.

Beyond technical contributions, Stallman established the Free Software Foundation and authored the GNU General Public License (GPL), one of the most widely used free software licenses. His advocacy has influenced countless developers, organizations, and governments worldwide.
